1.Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum,Pune

Just like people collect books, Raja Dinkar Kelkar had a penchant for artifacts and knick-knacks. This led him to collect approximately 20,000 articles ranging from furniture, vessels, and hookahs to puppet jewelry of the rarest kind. Dedicated to his son, the museum today also constitutes the Mastani Mahal and the famous Institute of Musicology and fine arts. On a fine day, if you take a stroll along the displays of the Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum Pune, you will find utensils dating back to the 18th and 19th century. Do stop and check for the intricate detailing on these rare finds made of copper, brass, and terracotta.

  • Location: Natu Baug,off Bajirao Road,Pune
  • Happy Hours - 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM
  • Entry Fees:INR 20 for adults,INR 200 for foreigners

2.National War Museum, Pune

Located in the cantonment area of Indian Army, the National War Museum was opened in the year 1998. One of the best museums in Pune, the fact that it is built and maintained by funds raised by the public makes it one unique place to visit in Pune. Dedicated to the brave martyrs who laid down their lives for the freedom of our country, the National War Museum simply gives you goosebumps. The gloomy story behind the fight of each soldier can be found here. The stone plaques rising to 8 feet have a total of 1200 names inscribed on them. However, the major attraction of this must-visit museum has to be the MIG 23 BN Jet Fighter which was used in the Kargil war.

  • Timings: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM

3.Tribal Museum, Pune

The indigenous tribes of India are integral to knowing its culture. Thus, if you are visiting Pune and don’t stop at the Tribal Museum, you will miss out on a lot. Located on Queen’s Road, this museum is part of the Tribal Research and Training Institute. Established in the year 1962 with the artifacts and other things of cultural importance being showcased from the year 1965, the museum is one of the few which preserves the ‘artistic and poetic impulses of the tribals of Maharashtra’. Some of the artifacts you can find here are clothes, utensils, bamboo works, masks, household articles, weapons

  • Location: Band Garden road
  • Timings: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM
  • Entry Fees:INR 10 for adults,INR 200 for foreigners

4.Shivaji Maharaj museum,Pune

A private historical museum with a digital library that is accessible to everyone, the mission of the Shivaji Maharaj Museum, Pune is to educate everyone about the real heroes of the past. With so many empires, states and cultures, the authentic facts are bound to get lost somewhere. But, for those who have a penchant of historical facts, relics, and other documents, this museum will help you acquaint yourself with the right knowledge.
